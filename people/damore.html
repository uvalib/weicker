Thomas D’Amore

Thomas D’Amore first worked in politics for former Connecticut Governor Thomas Meskill. In 1975 he left politics and returned to private life and the insurance and real estate business he founded. He came back to politics to manage Senator Lowell Weicker’s 1982 re-election campaign in which Weicker defeated Prescott Bush for the Republican nomination, then defeated Democrat Toby Moffett in the general election. Following that success, D’Amore served as Chairman of the Connecticut Republican party from 1983 to 1987. D’Amore did not, however, work in a managing position in 1988 due to personal commitments.

When Weicker ran for Governor in 1990, D’Amore broke from the Republican Party as well, and worked as campaign chairman and manager. After Weicker’s election, D’Amore became director of the transition, helping to hire and coordinate Weicker’s gubernatorial office staff. D’Amore then briefly served as Governor Weicker’s chief of staff before taking a six-month fellowship at the John F. Kennedy School of Government Institute of Politics at Harvard University in 1992. In 1996, D’Amore entered politics again, as a senior campaign advisor for Richard Lamm’s failed bid for the Reform Party nomination. In 2006, D’Amore worked for Ned Lamont’s campaign for the Senate. Lamont defeated Joseph Lieberman in the Democratic Party primary, but lost the congressional seat to Lieberman, who ran as an Independent in the general election.
